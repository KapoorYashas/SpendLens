# SpendLens — Go-To-Market Plan

## Target User

**Exact persona:** Engineering manager or CTO at a 10–50 person Series A SaaS startup who approves the monthly tooling budget but reviews AWS/GCP bills more carefully than SaaS line items. They probably know their AWS spend to the dollar but couldn't tell you their GitHub Copilot seat count without checking Stripe.

**What they Google:**
- "cursor vs github copilot cost comparison"
- "chatgpt team vs enterprise pricing"
- "reduce ai tools budget startup"
- "how much does claude pro cost for a team"

**Where they hang out:**
- r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups (technical leadership discussions)
- Hacker News (Show HN, Ask HN threads about tooling decisions)
- Lenny's Newsletter Slack (#tools-and-resources channel)
- Local YC founder Telegram/WhatsApp groups
- IndieHackers "I built a thing" forum section
- X/Twitter (following @swyx, @levelsio, @sama, discussing AI tool costs)

---

## First 100 Users, $0 Budget, 30 Days

### Day 1
Post on Hacker News: **"Show HN: SpendLens — free AI spend auditor for startups"**. Target: 200 upvotes, ~800 visits (HN front page typically drives 500-2,000 visits for tools). Key to getting traction: post at 8am ET on a Tuesday or Wednesday, write a comment immediately after posting explaining the product's origin ("I kept seeing engineering managers pay for both Cursor and Copilot for the same team"), respond to every comment within 30 minutes.

### Days 2–5
DM 20 founders and engineering managers on X who've tweeted about AI tool costs in the past 30 days. Search: `"copilot" OR "cursor" "per seat" since:2026-04-25`. Message: "Hey, I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend and finds savings — took me 2 minutes to find $180/month I was wasting. Would love your honest feedback: [URL]". Target: 15% reply rate = 3 new users.

### Day 7
Submit to **Ben's Bites** and **The Neuron** newsletters via their free submission forms. Both have 100k+ subscribers in the AI/founder audience. Lead with the specific savings number ("finds $X/month in savings in 2 minutes, no signup"). Target: 200 visits if picked up.

### Day 10
Post in **Lenny's Newsletter Slack** #tools-and-resources channel. Post genuine context: "Built this after noticing that our team was paying for Cursor AND Copilot for the same 3 devs. Made it free and public. Would appreciate feedback from anyone managing AI tooling budgets." No spam — provide value, mention it once.

### Day 14
Post in **IndieHackers** "I built a thing" thread with full transparency: monthly spend, how it works, what I learned building it. Attach a screenshot of a real audit result. IndieHackers community rewards honesty about unit economics.

### Day 20
Reach out to 5 Credex existing customers for testimonials. These are companies already in the Credex funnel who understand AI credit pricing — they're likely the most credible voices for "yes, I found savings with this."

## Unfair Channel

**Credex's existing sales motion:** Every Credex sales call surfaces a company that doesn't know their AI spend breakdown. Credex reps can now send SpendLens *before* every outbound call: "Before we talk, run your free audit here — takes 2 minutes." The prospect arrives to the call with a personalized savings report. This turns SpendLens into a pre-call qualification and warm-up tool for every Credex rep, with zero incremental marketing spend.

This channel is defensible because it requires a direct relationship with the sales team — competitors can't replicate it just by copying the product.
